Mythological Places:


Some places exist only in myths, but their stories live on!, including:

⁂ Great Wall of China (China): A colossal defensive structure built over centuries, showcasing ancient Chinese military architecture and engineering prowess.


⁂Mount Olympus (Greek Mythology): The home of the Greek gods, ruled by Zeus, where immortals feasted and controlled the fate of humans.


⁂Valhalla (Norse Mythology) : A majestic hall in Asgard where Viking warriors who died in battle were taken to feast and prepare for Ragnarok.


⁂Pyramids of Giza (Egypt): The only remaining wonder of the ancient world, these iconic pyramids are the tombs of pharaohs and a testament to ancient Egyptian engineering and power.


⁂Taj Mahal (India): A marble mausoleum built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan for his wife, Mumtaz Mahal, known for its exquisite beauty and intricate design.

Famous Myths & Legends:


Myths are timeless tales blending history and fantasy, shaping cultures and imaginations.

⁂The Epic of Gilgamesh (Mesopotamian): This ancient Mesopotamian epic, often considered the oldest surviving work of literature, tells the story of King Gilgamesh's quest for immortality and his friendship with the wild man Enkidu.


⁂The Trojan War (Greek): While a historical conflict is believed to have occurred, the myths surrounding the Trojan War, including the Iliad and Odyssey, focus on the gods' involvement, heroic figures like Achilles and Hector, and the legendary events of the war.


⁂The Story of Echo (Greek):This myth explains the origin of echoes, telling the story of a nymph cursed to only repeat what others say, eventually fading into a mere voice.


⁂The Story of Namazu (Japanese): According to Japanese lore, a giant catfish named Namazu is buried beneath Japan, and when it moves, earthquakes occur.


⁂The Story of Teonimanu (Solomon Islands): The Teonimanu myth from the Solomon Islands tells of a lost island that sank into the sea after a jealous husband, Roraimenu, used a wave curse against it, following his wife's elopement.

Interactive structures:


Discover interactive mythological structures that reveal ancient tales and legends!

⁂Pyramids of Giza, Egypt : These monumental structures, built around 4,500 years ago, served as tombs for pharaohs, and are the only Seven Wonders of the Ancient World still standing.


⁂Parthenon, Greece :Perched majestically atop the Acropolis in Athens, the Parthenon is an enduring symbol of classical Greek architecture and cultural sophistication.


⁂Wieliczka Salt Mine, Poland:The mine’s extensive network of chambers, each with a story to tell, offers a captivating journey through time and showcases the resourcefulness and creativity of generations of miners.


⁂The Eiffel Tower: a wrought-iron tower in Paris, France, was built for the 1889 World's Fair and is now a symbol of the city and a popular tourist attraction, with over 7 million visitors annually.


⁂Church of the Light in Osaka, Japan.: a minimalist concrete church located in Ibaraki, a suburb of Osaka, Japan, known for its cruciform slit in the wall that allows sunlight to create a powerful visual and spiritual effect
